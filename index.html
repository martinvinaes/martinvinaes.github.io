 CV       Research       Teaching     

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Political Science at Aarhus University. I got my Ph.D. in 2018 from the University of Copenhagen. My dissertation focused on electoral accountability and economic voting. You can read it here. I am also a founding member of the Behavioral and Experimental Public Administration Lab (BEPAL).

My primary field of interest is in comparative political behavior, with a focus on electoral accountability and on how voters assign responsibility for the quality of public services. In addition to this I am also interested in whether we can help citizens make better decisions using simple scalable interventions. Some of my research is published or forthcoming in the Political Science Research and Methods, Electoral Studies and Journal of Common Market Studies. My research and have been featured in a number of Danish News media outlets.

In addition to research I do a fair bit of writing and analysis the intersection between politics and research. Primarily in Danish. Off duty, I am an avid listener of podcasts, watcher of TV shows and reader of books about American history.

I am a Graduate Student in the Department of Political Science at the University of Copenhagen, finishing my disseration on electoral accountability and economic voting. In the beginning of 2018 I will be joining the Department of Political Science at Aarhus University./a> I am also a founding member of the Behavioral and Experimental Public Administration Lab (BEPAL).

My primary field of interest is in comparative political behavior, with a focus on electoral accountability and on how voters assign responsibility for the quality of public services. In addition to this I am also interested in the role of information in EU elections. Some of my research is published or forthcoming in the Political Science Research and Methods, Electoral Studies and Journal of Common Market Studies. My research and have been featured in a number of Danish News media outlets.

In addition to research I do a fair bit of writing on the intersection between politics and research. Primarily in Danish. Recently I have had op-eds in Berlingske, Politiken and Ræson). Off duty, I am avid listener of podcasts, watcher of TV shows and reader of books about American history.

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Political Science at Aarhus University. I got my Ph.D. in 2018 from the University of Copenhagen. My dissertation focused on electoral accountability and economic voting. You can read it here.

My primary field of interest is in comparative political behavior, with a focus on electoral accountability, citizen competence and the political consequenses of housing markets. In relation to this, I am interested in whether we can help citizens make more democratically competent decisions using simple scalable interventions. Some of my research is published or forthcoming in The American Political Science Review, The Journal of Politics, Political Behavior and Political Science Research and Methods. My research has been featured in a number of Danish news media outlets.

Off duty, I am an avid listener of podcasts, watcher of TV shows and reader of books about American history. I also have a dog and a toddler to take care off.
